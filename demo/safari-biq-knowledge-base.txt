1. COMPANY OVERVIEW
-------------------

Safari BIQ is a mid-size Kenyan safari and tourism operator headquartered in
Nairobi. We specialize in bespoke wildlife safari packages across Kenya's premier
national parks and conservancies, as well as coastal beach holidays, birdwatching
expeditions, cultural tours, and corporate team-building experiences.

We serve three primary customer segments:
- International tourists (60% of revenue) — primarily from Europe, North America,
  and Asia. These clients typically book 2-6 months in advance and favour premium
  multi-day itineraries.
- Corporate clients (25%) — Kenyan and multinational companies booking team
  retreats, conference safaris, and executive experiences. Lead time: 2-4 weeks.
- Domestic travelers (15%) — Kenyan residents booking weekend getaways, family
  holidays, and day trips. Lead time: 1-2 weeks.

Our competitive advantages:
- Expert Maasai and local guides with 10+ years of experience each
- Eco-tourism certifications (Ecotourism Kenya Gold rating)
- Bespoke itinerary design — no cookie-cutter packages
- Three owned safari lodges (Mara, Amboseli, Diani) with partner network of 12+ properties
- 25-vehicle safari fleet (Toyota Land Cruisers, extended-roof minivans, custom 4x4s)
- Approximately 120 staff across guiding, lodge operations, and HQ admin


2. DESTINATIONS & PARKS
-----------------------

2.1 Masai Mara National Reserve
- Location: Narok County, southwest Kenya
- Best for: Big Five, Great Migration (July–October), predator action
- Our lodges: Mara Savanna Tented Camp (owned, 20 tents)
- Peak season: July–October (wildebeest migration), December–March
- Key experience: Dawn game drives, Mara River crossings, balloon safaris
- Distance from Nairobi: 270 km (~5 hours by road, 45 min by charter flight)

2.2 Amboseli National Park
- Location: Kajiado County, at the foot of Mt Kilimanjaro
- Best for: Elephants (largest herds in Kenya), Kilimanjaro views
- Our lodges: Amboseli Sunset Lodge (owned, 15 rooms)
- Peak season: June–October, January–March
- Key experience: Kilimanjaro sunrise, elephant tracking, Maasai cultural visit
- Distance from Nairobi: 240 km (~4 hours)

2.3 Tsavo East & West National Parks
- Location: Coast/Eastern Province — Kenya's largest park system
- Best for: Red elephants, diverse landscapes, fewer crowds
- Peak season: June–October, January–February
- Key experience: Mzima Springs, Shetani Lava Flow, Lugard Falls
- Distance from Nairobi: 330 km to Tsavo West (~5 hours)

2.4 Samburu National Reserve
- Location: Samburu County, northern Kenya
- Best for: "Special Five" (Grevy's zebra, reticulated giraffe, Somali ostrich,
  gerenuk, Beisa oryx) — species not found in southern parks
- Peak season: June–October, January–February
- Distance from Nairobi: 350 km (~6 hours)

2.5 Laikipia Plateau / Conservancies
- Location: Central Kenya (Ol Pejeta, Lewa, Borana, Il Ngwesi)
- Best for: Rhino tracking (black & white), walking safaris, camel treks,
  night drives, horse-riding safaris, community conservancy model
- Peak season: Year-round (private conservancies, no seasonal closure)
- Unique: Most intimate safari experience — max 6-12 guests per camp

2.6 Lake Nakuru National Park
- Location: Rift Valley, Nakuru County
- Best for: Flamingos, rhino sanctuary, tree-climbing lions
- Often combined with Lake Naivasha (hippos, boat rides, Hell's Gate cycling)
- Distance from Nairobi: 160 km (~3 hours)

2.7 Kenyan Coast (Diani, Watamu, Lamu)
- Diani Beach: White-sand beach, water sports, reef snorkeling. Our property:
  Diani Coral Resort (owned, 12 rooms)
- Watamu: Marine National Park, whale sharks (Oct–March), turtle conservation
- Lamu: UNESCO World Heritage old town, dhow sailing, Swahili culture
- Typically offered as a "bush & beach" combo with safari

6. PRICING & SEASONALITY
-------------------------

Peak Season (highest demand, highest rates):
- July–October: Great Migration in the Mara
- December 15 – January 10: Christmas/New Year holidays
- Pricing: Standard rates (as listed above) — no discounts

Shoulder Season (moderate demand):
- January–March: Dry, good game viewing, moderate crowds
- November: Short rains — brief afternoon showers, lush scenery
- Pricing: 10-15% discount on standard rates

Low Season (lowest demand, best value):
- April–June: Long rains — some roads impassable, lodges may close
- Pricing: 25-40% discount, some "Green Season Specials"

Dynamic pricing factors:
- Group size: 10+ guests = 5% discount; 20+ = 10%
- Repeat customers: 5% loyalty discount
- Corporate: Negotiated rates (typically 8-12% off standard)
- Advance booking (90+ days out): 5% early bird discount
- Last-minute (< 7 days): May offer unsold inventory at 20% off

11. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
-------------------------------

Q: What is the best time to visit the Masai Mara?
A: July to October for the Great Migration. January to March for calving season
   (Ndutu/southern Serengeti side is Tanzania, but Mara is excellent year-round
   for Big Five).

Q: Are safaris safe for children?
A: Yes! We offer family-friendly safaris for children 5+. Private vehicle
   safaris are recommended for families with young children. Some walking
   safaris have a minimum age of 12.

Q: What should I pack for a safari?
A: Neutral-colored clothing (khaki, olive, tan), layers for cool mornings,
   binoculars, sunscreen (SPF 50+), insect repellent, camera with zoom lens,
   hat, closed walking shoes, and a reusable water bottle.

Q: Do I need vaccinations?
A: Yellow fever certificate required for entry into Kenya from endemic
   countries. Recommended: Typhoid, Hepatitis A & B, and anti-malaria
   prophylaxis (consult your travel doctor).

Q: Is Wi-Fi available on safari?
A: Our owned lodges have Wi-Fi in common areas. Remote camps may have limited
   or no connectivity — we consider this a feature, not a bug!

Q: Can I charge my devices on safari?
A: Yes, our luxury 4x4s have USB charging ports. Lodges have power (some on
   solar with generator backup). Bring a universal adapter (UK Type G plugs).
